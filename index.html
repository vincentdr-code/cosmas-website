The Problem

Manual inspection is failing at scale

Surgical instrument manufacturers rely on human visual inspection to catch defects before instruments reach hospitals. The evidence shows it doesn't work well enough.

01

Fatigue degrades accuracy

Inspector performance drops measurably after two hours. The same defect passes the morning shift and fails the afternoon shift. There is no reliable, reproducible baseline.

02

Micro-defects are invisible to the naked eye

Cracks under 0.1mm, early-stage corrosion, and surface porosity — the defects most likely to cause instrument failure in the OR — require magnification and controlled lighting that manual inspection cannot consistently apply at line speed.

03

Audit trails are built on paper

21 CFR Part 820 and the new QMSR (effective February 2, 2026) require retrieval-ready, immutable, event-linked inspection records. Manual paper logs cannot satisfy this standard under FDA inspection.

04

Recall costs dwarf prevention costs

A single major recall costs an average of $600M in liability, remediation, and brand damage. The hardware to deploy Cosmas Sentry on one production line costs $203K all-in. The math is not complicated.

How It Works

From the camera to the audit log in under 40ms

Cosmas Sentry is a five-stage inspection pipeline that runs entirely on your production floor — no cloud, no data egress, no integration with external servers.

01 📷
Capture
Basler acA2040 industrial camera captures the instrument at 24fps. Structured lighting eliminates reflective noise on stainless steel surfaces.
BASLER ACA2040 · 2MP · 24FPS
02 ⚡
Infer
YOLOv8 model — pre-trained on surgical instruments — runs on NVIDIA Jetson Orin NX. Five defect classes, sub-40ms inference latency.
YOLOV8 · NVIDIA JETSON ORIN NX · <40MS
03 ⚖️
Decide
Pass or fail verdict with per-class confidence scores. Detection threshold calibrated to your specification during the 30-day pilot.
99.3% BENCHMARK ACCURACY · SURGSCAN AI 2025
04 🔧
Reject
PLC signal via Modbus TCP triggers the Allen-Bradley conveyor reject mechanism in real time. No human in the loop required at this stage.
ALLEN-BRADLEY · MODBUS TCP · REAL-TIME
05 📋
Log
Every inspection event writes an immutable record: inspection ID, timestamp, image hash, model version, verdict, and operator ID — mapped to QMSR §820.45 DHR requirements.
